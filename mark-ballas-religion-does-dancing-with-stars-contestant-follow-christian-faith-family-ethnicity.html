Mark Ballas Religion: Does Dancing With Stars Contestant Follow Christian Faith? Family Ethnicity

Mark Ballas’s religion has been a mystery to his fans after they wonder which faith he follows.

Mark was named “Performer of the Year” in 2005. He won the British Open to the World, the US Open to the World, and the International Open to the World titles. 

Ballas appeared as a Hufflepuff House extra in Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone. 

Mark Ballas Religion: His Family Background Explored

Mark Ballas’s religion is Christianity, and his nationality is American.

Ballas was born in Texas, United States, on May 24, 1986.

He is the son of Corky and Shirley Ballas, both dancers. His paternal ancestors were of Mexican and Greek descent, and his mother was born in the United Kingdom and is primarily of English descent. His mother is likewise of Malagasy descent from Madagascar. 

George Ballas, his paternal grandpa, invented the Weed Eater lawn-trimming equipment. Karolos (“Charles”) Ballas and Maria Lymnaos, his paternal great-grandparents, were Greek immigrants to the United States.

Mark Ballas Wife: Who Is BC Jean?

Ballas, together with his wife, BC Jean, is a part of the singer-songwriter duo Alexander Jean. Both of them became engaged in November 2015 after three years of dating. 

They got married on November 25, 2016, in Malibu, California.

His wife, Brittany Jean Carlson, better known by her stage name BC Jean, is a singer-songwriter and actor from the United States.

She is well recognized for writing and performing the song “If I Were a Boy,” which helped Beyoncé reach worldwide commercial success.

Mark Ballas Career Details Revealed

In South London, Ballas attended Rosemead Preparatory School. At the age of 11, Mark was accepted into the Italia Conti Academy of Theatre Arts in London on a full scholarship. 

Ballas won the Junior Latin American Dance Championship and the gold medal at the Junior Olympics with his former partner Julianne Hough.

He was the lead dancer in the UK national tour of the Spanish musical Maria de Buenos Aires and portrayed the lead role of Tony in the musical Copacabana. 

He was also the understudy for Ritchie Valens in Buddy, The Buddy Holly Story’s UK national tour. 

Ballas made a guest appearance on Samantha Who’s season debut in October 2008.

On September 6, 2016, it was revealed that Ballas would be the last actor to play Frankie Valli in the Broadway production of Jersey Boys before it closed in 2017. On May 26, 2018, it was reported that he would play the Teen Angel in the 2018 Toronto revival of Grease, which would run from June 7 to June 10, 2018.

Was Mark Ballas In Dancing With The Stars?

Ballas was paired with Cheetah Girls star Sabrina Bryan on the fifth season of Dancing with the Stars. On October 30, 2007, the couple was voted off the show. They are the only couple who have been invited back for an exhibition dance.

Olympic gold medalist figure skater Kristi Yamaguchi was Ballas’ partner on Dancing with the Stars season 6. They were the season champions.

ABC announced the cast of the seventh season of Dancing with the Stars on August 25, 2008, with Ballas paired with reality television sensation Kim Kardashian. On September 30, 2008, they were the third pair eliminated, finishing in eleventh place.

For season 16, he was teamed with two-time Olympic champion Aly Raisman. Aly and Mark were allowed to become finalists since season 16 saw four couples reach the finals for the first time. 

For season 25, Ballas returned as a professional dancer and was paired with musician Lindsey Stirling. Ballas and Stirling advanced to the finals and finished second.

For season 31, Ballas returned to the show and was paired with dancer and social media star Charli D’Amelio.
