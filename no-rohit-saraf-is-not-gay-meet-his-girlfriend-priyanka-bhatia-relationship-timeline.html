Rohit Saraf Career

Rohit Saraf was born on 8 December 1996 in Kathmandu, Nepal, into an Indian family. He moved back with his family to Delhi when he was five. He was raised in Delhi but moved to Mumbai when he started modeling.

His Father, Suresh Saraf, died when Rohit was 12 years old. He completed his early education at the Saint Francis D’Assisit High School. He then went to St. Xavier’s College, Mumbai, for graduation.

Saraf’s television career began with several programs, including the teen drama Best Friends Forever? on Channel V. He also appeared in the short film “Medium” shown in school theaters.

In the coming-of-age movie Dear Zindagi, he made his acting debut as the younger brother of Alia Bhatt’s character (2016).

Then, in the Norwegian drama What Will People Say, which was chosen as Norway’s entry for the Best Foreign Language Film at the 91st Academy Awards, Saraf played the cousin of the lead character.

In the comedy-drama Hichki, produced by Yash Raj Films and starring Rani Mukerji, Saraf made an appearance. Then he appeared in the biographical drama The Sky Is Pink with Priyanka Chopra and Zaira Wasim (2019).

Following that, Saraf joined a large cast in Anurag Basu’s Ludo. He also appeared in the Netflix romance drama series Mismatched with Prajakta Koli.

He later made his Tamil debut in the love drama Kamali From Nadukkaveri, where he starred opposite Anandhi.

His most recent appearance was in the anthology Feels Like Ishq, a July 2021 Netflix exclusive. In the short film Star Host, directed by Anand Tiwari, he plays the part of House host Aditya.
